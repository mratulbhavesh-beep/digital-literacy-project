Nowadays being good with technology is really important. This project was part of the Digital Literacy course. The goal of this project is to teach students how to use tools in a way and be responsible when using them.

As a Student Digital Ambassador my job was to look at parts of literacy. I made resources to help other students learn more about using technology and feel more confident when using it.

This project has five parts. Each part looks at something

* making pictures and videos to communicate

* building a professional portfolio

* working with others online

* being polite when talking to people online

* and being aware of cybercrime.

I got to try out tools like Canva, GitHub, LinkedIn, HackerRank and Google Workspace.

Importantly I learned how to use these tools when I am studying. When I have a job.

This project helped me learn how to use platforms in a productive and professional way.

Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic (Module 1)

The first task was to understand what digital literacy is and show it in a picture.

I made an infographic using Canva, a tool thats easy to use and has templates to make things look good.

The infographic talked about things like:

* What digital literacy means

* Why it is important to be safe on the internet

* What digital tools are useful for students

* How to make a presence online

When I was working on this part I also looked at different tools for making presentations and designing things, including:

* Canva for making posters and infographics

* Prezi for making presentations

* Miro for making boards that people can work on together

* CoolText for making text look cool

* GIFs for making content more fun and interesting

When I was designing the infographic I tried to balance the words and the pictures.

One hard thing was choosing the points without making the design look too busy.

I realized that communicating ideas in a way is about showing the information clearly not about adding more stuff.

This task helped me get better at communicating my ideas in a way.

Task 4: Email Etiquette & Social Media Etiquette (Module 4)

This part was about communicating with people in a way which's an important skill for students.

I wrote two emails:

* Asking for time to finish an assignment

* Applying for an internship

When I was writing these emails I followed a structure:

* A clear and relevant subject line

* A formal greeting

* An organized body

* A closing and signature

Important things I learned include:

* Always being professional and polite

* Not using language or abbreviations

* Keeping messages clear and short

* Respecting the person I am writing to

I also made a list of things to do. Not to do on social media.

Some important points:

**Do’s**

* Share content

* Keep my privacy settings safe

* Think before posting

**Don’ts**

* Do not share sensitive information

* Do not post offensive content

* Do not spread false information

A common problem is sending emails that are not clear or are too informal to professors, which can lead to misunderstandings.

This part showed me how important it is to communicate in a way to make an impression.

Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention (Module 5)

This task was about understanding cybercrime and how to be safe online.

I studied types of cybercrimes such as:

* Phishing attacks

* Online payment fraud

* Identity theft

* Cyberbullying

This part also explained:

* How cybercrimes happen step-by-step

* Who is usually targeted

* The impact on individuals and society

I learned that financial frauds are common and often happen because people do not know enough.

Many people share their passwords click on links without thinking.

I made a list of things to do to prevent cybercrime, including:

* Never sharing my passwords or OTPs

* Avoiding links and messages

* Using trusted payment apps

* Updating my passwords regularly

* Reporting fraud on the cybercrime website

* Calling the helpline in case of fraud

This task helped me become more aware of the risks online and encouraged me to be safe when using digital platforms.

Through the tasks I learned how to use tools effectively build a professional presence and communicate in a professional way in academic and professional settings.

I also became more aware of the dangers. The importance of being careful when using digital platforms.

The things I learned from this project will help me during my time in college and in my career.

Overall this project made me more confident, in using technology in a way. Inspired me to keep improving my digital skills.
